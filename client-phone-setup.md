# Connecting Your Phone — Setup Guide

Welcome! This guide walks you through connecting your business phone to your new
auto-text system. If a customer calls and nobody answers, they'll automatically
receive a friendly text from you — so no lead ever goes cold.

There are **two ways** to set this up. We recommend Option A for most businesses.

---

## Option A — Recommended: Conditional Call Forwarding

**What this means:** Your existing business number stays exactly the same. Customers
call the number they already know. If no one picks up after a few rings, your phone
carrier silently forwards the call to your new system, which answers it and sends
the auto-text.

**Your customers won't notice any difference** — they still dial the same number
they always have. If you answer, the call connects normally and nothing happens.
The auto-text only triggers when the call goes unanswered.

### How to turn it on

You'll need the **Twilio phone number** we set up for you. It looks like a regular
US phone number (e.g., `+1 (512) 555-0192`). We'll call it your **forwarding
number** below.

Pick your carrier below and follow the steps. These codes are dialed directly from
your phone — no app needed.

---

#### AT&T

1. Open your phone's dialer (as if you're making a call)
2. Dial: `*92` followed by your **forwarding number**, then press **Call**
   - Example: `*925125550192` → Call
3. You should hear a confirmation tone or message

To turn it off later, dial `*93` and press Call.

---

#### Verizon

1. Open your phone's dialer
2. Dial: `*71` followed by your **forwarding number**, then press **Call**
   - Example: `*715125550192` → Call
3. Wait for the confirmation

To turn it off later, dial `*73` and press Call.

---

#### T-Mobile

1. Open your phone's dialer
2. Dial: `**61*` followed by your **forwarding number** and then `#`, then press **Call**
   - Example: `**61*5125550192#` → Call
3. Wait for the confirmation

To turn it off later, dial `##61#` and press Call.

---

#### Using a VoIP or business phone system? (RingCentral, Google Voice, Grasshopper, etc.)

The steps above are for standard cell phones. If your business number runs through a
VoIP service or a separate phone system, forwarding is set up inside that service's
app or dashboard — not by dialing a code. Look for a setting called **"Call
Forwarding"** or **"No Answer Forwarding"** in your account. If you're not sure
where to find it, contact your provider's support team and ask:

> *"How do I set up conditional call forwarding to an outside number when I don't
> answer?"*

---

### What to expect after setup (Option A)

- **Nothing changes for your customers.** They call the same number as always.
- **If you answer:** The call connects normally. No text is sent.
- **If you don't answer in time:** The call is forwarded, a brief hold message plays
  ("Thanks for calling — please hold while we connect you"), and if the connection
  still isn't made, the caller automatically receives a text from your business
  within a few seconds.
- **The auto-text says:** *"Hey! Sorry we missed you — this is [Your Business Name].
  What can we help with? Reply here or book a time: [your booking link]"*
- Customers can reply to that text and you'll see it on your phone.

---

## Option B — Replace Your Listed Number with the Twilio Number

**What this means:** Instead of keeping your old number, you start using the Twilio
number as your primary business number everywhere. Calls go straight into the system
and are forwarded to your phone. The auto-text fires on any missed call.

This approach is simpler technically but requires more legwork on your end, because
you'll need to update your number everywhere it appears:

- [ ] **Google Business Profile** — update your phone number in your listing
- [ ] **Your website** — update any phone number links or text
- [ ] **Social media profiles** (Facebook, Instagram, Yelp, etc.)
- [ ] **Printed materials** — business cards, flyers, signage (these will be wrong
      until reprinted)
- [ ] **Any directories or ads** you're listed in

> **We generally don't recommend this option** unless you're a brand-new business
> that hasn't published a number yet, or you're already planning to change your number.
> Option A lets you keep everything exactly as it is.
